About CUSUM Charts in Healthcare

What is a CUSUM?

The Cumulative Sum (CUSUM) chart is a sequential analysis method that accumulates evidence of a process change over time. For binary outcomes (success/failure), each case contributes a score based on the log-likelihood ratio between the target rate p0 and the alert rate p1.

When the cumulative sum crosses a decision threshold h, it signals that the failure rate has likely shifted above the acceptable level. The CUSUM resets to zero whenever it would go negative (one-sided) or can be run two-sided.

The weight w for a failure is log(p1/p0), and for a success is log((1-p1)/(1-p0)). A failure adds a positive score (evidence of deterioration) and a success subtracts (evidence of acceptable performance).

Threshold Selection

The threshold h controls the trade-off between sensitivity (detecting real shifts quickly) and specificity (avoiding false alarms). Higher thresholds reduce false alarms but delay detection.

The Average Run Length (ARL) measures expected cases until signal: ARL0 is the false-alarm rate under p0; ARL1 is the detection speed under p1.

Clinical Applications

CUSUM monitoring is widely used in surgery and critical care:

  • Monitoring surgeon learning curves for new procedures
  • Detecting deterioration in cardiac surgery outcomes
  • Post-operative infection surveillance
  • ICU mortality monitoring
  • Transplant center quality oversight
