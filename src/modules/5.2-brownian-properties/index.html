About Brownian Motion Properties

What is Brownian Motion?

Brownian motion (or Wiener process) is a continuous-time stochastic process with independent, stationary, Gaussian increments. It is the scaling limit of random walks and the fundamental building block of stochastic calculus.

Self-Similarity

Brownian motion is self-similar with exponent 1/2: for any constant a > 0, the rescaled process has the same distribution as the original.

This means zooming into a Brownian path (rescaling time by a^2 and space by a) produces a statistically identical path. The roughness you see at every scale is a consequence of this property.
