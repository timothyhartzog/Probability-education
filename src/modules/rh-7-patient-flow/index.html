Probability Theory in the Rural Nexus

Urban hospitals benefit from the Law of Large Numbers (LLN). With hundreds of patients daily, the average arrival rate and service time are stable and highly predictable. The variance of the mean $\sigma^2/n$ approaches zero as $n$ increases.

In a Critical Access Hospital (CAH), $n$ is very small. Arrival patterns are "lumpy." A single high-acuity event (Chapter 7: The "Surge") can represent 500% of the daily mean demand, immediately saturating the facility or exceeding the "96-hour stay rule" (Chapter 6).
