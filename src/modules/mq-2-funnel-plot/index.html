About Funnel Plots in Healthcare

Why Funnel Plots?

Funnel plots are the gold standard for comparing institutional performance in healthcare. Unlike simple league tables or rankings, funnel plots account for the precision of each estimate by plotting outcomes against case volume.

Low-volume hospitals have wider confidence limits (the "funnel" shape), reflecting greater statistical uncertainty. This prevents false labeling of small hospitals as outliers due to random variation alone.

Interpreting the Funnel

Points inside the funnel represent hospitals whose outcomes are consistent with the national average, given their volume. Points outside the 95% limits warrant further investigation; those outside 99.8% limits are strong signals of genuinely different performance.

Overdispersion adjustment inflates the funnel to account for between-hospital heterogeneity beyond random variation (e.g., differences in case mix not captured by risk adjustment).

Statistical Methodology

For proportion data (mortality, readmission), the expected standard error at volume n is SE = √(p(1-p)/n), where p is the national rate. The funnel limits are p ± z·SE for the chosen confidence level.

With overdispersion parameter φ, the variance inflates to φ·p(1-p)/n, widening the funnel. φ is estimated from the between-hospital variation using a method-of-moments approach.

CMS & Quality Reporting

CMS uses funnel plot methodology (or equivalent) in programs like Hospital Compare, the Hospital Readmissions Reduction Program (HRRP), and the Hospital-Acquired Condition Reduction Program (HACRP). These methods inform value-based purchasing and public reporting.
