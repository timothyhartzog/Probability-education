Model Description

In Schelling's model, agents of two types occupy cells on a grid. Each agent is happy if at least a threshold fraction of its neighbors share its type. Unhappy agents relocate to random empty cells. Even low thresholds (e.g., 30%) lead to high macro-level segregation — a striking example of emergent behavior from local rules.
