About Markov Chains

Definition

A Markov chain is a stochastic process where the future state depends only on the current state, not on the history. Formally, for states X0, X1, ...:

The transition matrix P encodes all transition probabilities: entry Pij is the probability of moving from state i to state j in one step.

Stationary Distribution

A distribution π is stationary if it satisfies πP = π. Under certain conditions (irreducibility, aperiodicity), the chain converges to π regardless of the initial distribution.

Watch the distribution evolution panel: the bars converge to the dashed overlay as n increases. The total variation distance measures how far the current distribution is from stationarity.

Convergence and Mixing

The mixing time is the number of steps needed for the chain to be close to its stationary distribution. Chains with absorbing states or periodic structure may not converge to a unique stationary distribution.

Try the different presets to see how chain structure affects convergence: the weather model converges quickly, the periodic chain oscillates forever, and the gambler's ruin gets absorbed.
