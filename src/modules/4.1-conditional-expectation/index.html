About Conditional Expectation

Conditional Expectation as Projection

Given a random variable X on a finite probability space (Ω, F, P) and a sub-σ-algebra G ⊆ F, the conditional expectation E[X|G] is the unique G-measurable random variable that satisfies the defining property:

Geometrically, E[X|G] is the orthogonal projection of X onto the subspace of G-measurable (square-integrable) functions in L²(Ω, F, P). It "flattens" X by averaging within each atom of G.
