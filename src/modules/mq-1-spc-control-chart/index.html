About SPC Control Charts in Healthcare

What is Statistical Process Control?

Statistical Process Control (SPC) applies statistical methods to monitor and control healthcare processes. A control chart plots observed values over time against statistically derived control limits, distinguishing common-cause variation (inherent to the process) from special-cause variation (signals of change).

Control limits are typically set at ±3σ from the center line, capturing 99.73% of values when the process is stable.

Chart Types

p-Chart: Monitors the proportion of events (e.g., infection rate). Control limits adjust for varying denominator sizes.

u-Chart: Monitors event rates per unit of exposure (e.g., falls per 1,000 patient-days). Similar to p-chart but for count rates.

X-bar Chart: Tracks the mean of subgroups over time.

Individuals Chart: Plots individual measurements when subgrouping is not practical. Uses moving range for sigma estimation.

Western Electric Rules

Beyond a single point outside control limits, the Western Electric rules detect subtler patterns of special-cause variation:

  • Rule 1: One point beyond 3σ
  • Rule 2: Two of three consecutive points beyond 2σ
  • Rule 3: Four of five consecutive points beyond 1σ
  • Rule 4: Eight consecutive points on one side of the center line
Healthcare Applications

SPC charts are mandated or recommended by CMS, The Joint Commission, and IHI for monitoring key quality indicators:

  • Hospital-acquired infection rates (CLABSI, CAUTI, SSI)
  • 30-day readmission and mortality rates
  • Patient falls and medication errors
  • ED wait times and length of stay
  • Surgical complications and never events
