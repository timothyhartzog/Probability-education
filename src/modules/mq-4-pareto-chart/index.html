About Pareto Analysis in Healthcare Quality

The Pareto Principle

The Pareto principle (80/20 rule) states that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes. In healthcare quality improvement, this means a small number of root causes typically drive the majority of adverse events, complaints, or errors.

Pareto charts combine a bar chart (sorted by frequency) with a cumulative percentage line to visually separate the "vital few" causes from the "trivial many."

How to Read the Chart

Bars show the count or frequency of each category, sorted from highest to lowest. The cumulative line shows the running total as a percentage. Where the line crosses 80%, you've identified the vital few categories responsible for most of the problem.

Focus improvement efforts on the leftmost bars — small changes to the top categories yield the greatest overall improvement.

Quality Improvement Applications
  • Root cause analysis: Prioritize which failure modes to address first
  • Patient safety: Focus on the most common types of adverse events
  • Patient experience: Target the top complaint categories
  • Medication safety: Identify the most frequent error types
  • Before/after comparison: Verify that targeted interventions reduced the vital few
