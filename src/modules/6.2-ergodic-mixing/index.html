About Ergodic Theory & Mixing

The Ergodic Theorem

Mixing Time

The mixing time is defined as the first time the total variation distance drops below a threshold (conventionally 1/4):

Mixing time quantifies how many steps the chain needs to "forget" its starting state. It is related to the spectral gap: for reversible chains, tmix is of order 1/γ where γ = 1 − |λ2|.

The Cutoff Phenomenon

Some families of Markov chains exhibit a cutoff phenomenon: the total variation distance stays close to 1 for many steps, then drops abruptly to near 0 over a narrow window. The barbell graph preset demonstrates a bottleneck effect where mixing is delayed until the chain crosses between the two clusters.

Reversibility & Detailed Balance

A chain is reversible with respect to π if it satisfies detailed balance: π(x)P(x,y) = π(y)P(y,x) for all states x, y. All preset chains in this module are reversible. Reversibility implies the transition matrix has real eigenvalues, and the spectral gap directly controls the rate of convergence.
