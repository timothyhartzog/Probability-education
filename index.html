Law of Large Numbers

The Law of Large Numbers states that as the number of trials increases, the sample average converges to the expected value. Formally, for i.i.d. random variables X1, X2, ..., Xn with mean μ:

P(|X̄n - μ| > ε) → 0 as n → ∞

Central Limit Theorem

The Central Limit Theorem states that the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as sample size increases, regardless of the population's distribution. For sample means X̄n from a population with mean μ and variance σ²:

√n (X̄n - μ) / σ ⟶ N(0, 1) as n → ∞

Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' Theorem describes how to update the probability of a hypothesis given new evidence:

P(H|E) = P(E|H) · P(H) / P(E)

where P(H) is the prior, P(E|H) is the likelihood, and P(H|E) is the posterior.
